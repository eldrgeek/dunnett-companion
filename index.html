The Book Group Birthday

A Renaissance folk ballad for Jan
Verse I

In Bruges where the dyers stain the cloth
And merchants count their gold
A woman turns eighty-three today
Whose heart has not grown old
She reads of Claes and his disguise
Of intrigue, trade, and art
And every Wednesday gathers friends
To take the story apart

Chorus

Happy birthday, Jan
From the dyer's boy and a digital mind
From a man in Denver who still can't find
The difference between who and whom
But he built you a present
He built you a room
Where Niccolò lives
And the maps all bloom

Verse II

From Trebizond to Timbuktu
From Iceland's frozen shore
She follows every thread that Dunnett spun
And always finds one more
So here's a gift we made for you
A world inside a screen
Where every road that Nicholas walked
Can finally be seen
